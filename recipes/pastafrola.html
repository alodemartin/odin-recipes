Pastafrola

Pasta frola, or pastafrola, is a traditional cake decorated with strips of dough and filled with sweets. Depending on where it is prepared, the filling may vary, but the most common in Argentina is the traditional quince pastafrola.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 cup of sugar (200 grams)
  • 2 eggs
  • 100 grams of butter
  • 1 Teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch of lemon zest
  • 400 grams of quince paste

Steps

  1. Mix the sugar with the butter in a bowl at room temperature. Then, add the eggs one by one and add the flour, vanilla essence and lemon or orange zest little by little.
  2. Pour the mixture onto a floured surface and knead until you obtain a soft dough. Let the traditional pastafrola dough rest in the refrigerator for approximately 30 minutes.
  3. After the resting time, remove the dough from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature. Then, grease and sprinkle a baking pan with flour. Spread the dough with your fingers, making the base for the easy homemade frola pasta.
  4. Cut the quince paste into small pieces and mash it with a little hot water or sweet wine until it softens completely. In this step you can use a potato masher or heat it a little to achieve the desired texture.
  5. Add the quince to the frola pasta dough base that you have already prepared, spread it well everywhere and cover it with strips of dough to decorate.
  6. Finally, bake the pasta frola with quince recipe at an average temperature of 180ÂºC for about 45 minutes.

Ready! Now you can enjoy this exquisite cake accompanied by your loved ones.
