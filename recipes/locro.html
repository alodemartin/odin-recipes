Locro

Based on pumpkin, white corn and potatoes, locro continues to be one of the favorite dishes to beat the cold of winter, tasted on Argentine national holidays and shared with the family. His recipe is one of the most popular in this country. Step by step, how to make traditional locro.

Ingredients

  • 400 grams of pork breast
  • 400 grams of roast beef
  • 2 eggs
  • 150 grams of smoked bacon
  • 1 red chorizo
  • 1 pork leg
  • 100 grams of pigskin
  • 600 grams of pumpkin
  • 1 onion
  • 150 grams of beans
  • 300 grams of cracked white corn
  • 1 liter of water
  • Salt
  • Oil

Ingredients for the sauce

  • 2 units of green onion
  • 1 tablespoon of ground chili
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Oil

Steps

    TIP: Stir the mixture sporadically to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  1. In a bowl, put the beans and fill the container with water until the beans have been covered.
  2. In another bowl, place the corn and also fill the container with water until they are covered. Let soak separately and refrigerate for 12 hours.
  3. Then, place the water in a pot and add the pork leg with the skin. Bring to a boil and cook for 5 minutes to remove impurities. Remove from water and reserve.
  4. Then, in a large pot (approximately 6 liters), place a little oil, the cut onion, the beans and the corn. Stir, cover with water and let cook for an hour.
  5. Cut the pumpkin into cubes, and the meats, bacon and chorizo ​​into pieces. Add everything to the pot, including the leather (previously cut) and the leg.
  6. Cover with water and cook for 2 hours.

How to make the spicy sauce

  1. Chop the green onion and fry in a frying pan over medium heat with a little oil along with the salt, ground chili and paprika.

Ready! Now you can enjoy this exquisite locro accompanied by your loved ones.
